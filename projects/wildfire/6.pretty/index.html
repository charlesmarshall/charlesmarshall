Wildfire CMS version 6.pretty

Although version 6 had many big architecture changes it was mostly designed on the fly by myself while I was building it, and it shows.

This time, an actual designer was involved. As it was a couple of years ago (think late 2011, early 2012) there are plently of shadows, gradients and rounded corners as that was in vogue at the time.

This darker look and feel gave a much slicker feel than previous iterations. With the large, chunky and brightly coloured buttons for primary actions we tried to give obvious signs of what is important on the page.

Not just a pretty face

After we'd been using Wildfire v6 we found a few ways to improve the speed a little as it was suffering with larger tree structures.

A few other small tweaks were made, like removing hard-coded class names & making use of the internal primary key name rather than directly calling it 'id' (there are still a few of those around to this day!)

Keeping it steady

Even though this is classed as a major release, it has maintained almost all of the same infrastructure as v6, giving it a nice stable & well tested code base.

With the swap over to tree structures on content and event based system inside the controllers, most future changes are planned to be smaller, but more frequent.

We may end up moving away from the traditional point release naming convention and go to hashs... we'll see.

The joy of extending

Now the new tree layout has a fair few miles under it's belt and is now the default setup for a couple of major versions it meant our plugins became easier to update & reuse.

The differences between a v6 site and a v6.pretty being small enough that most plugins built for one will work on the other. In the few cases where they don't the changes are normally simple enough to do.

Now there is a stable underpinning, we have quite a lot more plugins for more of the common functions needed. Covering off things such as sitemaps, archive style navigation and many more.
